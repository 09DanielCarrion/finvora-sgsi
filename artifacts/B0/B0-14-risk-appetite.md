# Risk Appetite and Certification Objectives (Clause 5.1, 6.2)

## Risk Appetite Statement (Approved by the Board, January 2026)

- Finvora accepts moderate operational risk as part of its growth process.
- Finvora has zero tolerance for customer data breaches that result in financial loss to its customers.
- Finvora has low tolerance for security incidents that affect the availability of the payments platform (maximum RTO: 4 hours).
- Finvora accepts low regulatory risk where the potential financial impact is below $50,000 USD in fines.
- Finvora has low tolerance for reputational risk; any public breach could damage the Hexalink relationship and the planned Series B fundraise.

## Risk Acceptance Criteria

| Risk Level | Score Range | Required Action |
|---|---|---|
| Low | 1-6 | Accepted, no immediate action required |
| Medium | 7-12 | Treatment plan required within 90 days |
| High | 13-19 | Treatment plan required within 30 days |
| Critical | 20-25 | Immediate action required, escalate to CEO |

## Certification Objectives

- **Primary objective:** Achieve ISO 27001 certification before December 31, 2026 (contractual requirement from Hexalink GmbH).
- **Secondary objective:** Establish a basic GDPR compliance program by Q3 2026.
- **Tertiary objective:** Document an EU AI Act compliance roadmap by Q4 2026.
- **2027 objective:** Pursue SOC 2 Type II to support expansion into the US market.

## Current Risk Posture

Isabella Torres (CISO) presented an informal risk assessment to the board in February 2026: "Finvora currently operates with a security risk profile I would classify as High. We have historical incidents that were never fully remediated, significant technical debt, and an absence of documented baseline controls. The good news is that our AWS cloud infrastructure gives us a solid foundation. The bad news is that we are not taking full advantage of it yet."
