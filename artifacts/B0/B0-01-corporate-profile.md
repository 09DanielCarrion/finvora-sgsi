### History

Finvora was founded in April 2019 by two former BBVA Continental bankers, Carlos Mendoza and a co-founder who exited the company in 2022. The original idea was a B2B payments platform that would let mid-sized Peruvian companies collect and pay invoices without depending entirely on traditional banking rails.

In 2021, Finvora pivoted toward digital credit. Using a proprietary AI-driven credit scoring model, the company began issuing credit lines to Peruvian SMEs that traditional banks considered too small or too risky to underwrite efficiently. This pivot is what drove the company's growth: digital credit now represents the majority of revenue.

In 2023, Finvora expanded regionally into Colombia and Chile, replicating the credit model with local data partnerships. The expansion has been slower than projected, and these markets still represent a small share of total revenue.

In 2024, Finvora signed a contract with Hexalink GmbH, a German supply-chain financing company, to provide embedded credit and payment APIs to Hexalink's network of suppliers. This contract is strategically important and brought Finvora, for the first time, under direct GDPR obligations and onto the radar of the EU AI Act, since Hexalink requires its financial technology partners to be ISO 27001 certified by the end of 2026.

### Mission, Vision, Values

**Mission:** To give underserved SMEs in Latin America fast, fair access to credit and payment infrastructure, using data and technology to replace outdated underwriting.

**Vision:** To become the leading embedded finance platform for SMEs across Latin America, trusted by both local businesses and international partners.

**Values:**
- Move fast, but document what you build
- Data-driven decisions over gut feeling
- Customer trust is the actual product
- Compliance is an enabler of growth, not a blocker (aspirational - not yet fully lived)

### Ownership Structure

Finvora closed a Series A round in 2022 led by Meridian Ventures, a US-based venture capital fund focused on Latin American fintech. Meridian Ventures holds 23% of the company and has one seat on Finvora's five-member board. The founders and early employees hold the majority of the remaining equity, with a smaller allocation reserved for an employee stock option pool.

## Strategic Objectives 2026-2027

- Achieve ISO 27001 certification before December 2026 (contractual requirement from Hexalink)
- Expand into Mexico in Q3 2026
- Launch a parametric insurance product in Q4 2026
- Raise a Series B round in 2027

## Key Risks (Board Perspective)

- Dependence on the Hexalink contract, which represents 18% of revenue and is the trigger for most current compliance pressure
- Increasing regulatory burden across all operating markets simultaneously
- Accumulated technical debt from rapid, under-governed growth
- High engineering turnover (30% annually), which erodes institutional knowledge, including security knowledge
